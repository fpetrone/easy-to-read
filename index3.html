What is the European Parliament and how does it work

The European Parliament

The European Parliament represents
450 million people who live in the European Union.

People in the European Union
vote for members of the Parliament.

Members of the European Parliament are called MEPs.
MEPs represent people. There are 705 MEPs.
MEPs look at plans for new European Union laws.
MEPs change laws to make them better.
MEPs check how the European Union is working.

What the European Parliament does

The Parliament makes
new laws with the Council.

The Parliament and the Council
also decide on the European Union budget.

How the European Parliament works

Groups

Most MEPs work in political groups.

Groups are made up of MEPs
from different countries
and political parties.

The groups have common aims.

Committees

The Parliament has 20 permanent committees.
All MEPs work in the committees.
They propose improvements to laws.
MEPs vote on these proposals.

Plenary sessions

Then the whole Parliament votes
on new laws in plenary sessions.

Plenaries bring all MEPs together.
MEPs debate and vote on new laws.

MEPs elect a president
to represent the Parliament.

The president also chairs
the plenaries.

The president is elected
for two and a half years.

Cast your vote

You can vote for people
to represent you in the Parliament.

European elections
take place every five years.
