What is the European Parliament and how does it work

The European Parliament

The Parliament represents
450 million people who live in the European Union.

The European Union is called the EU

People in the EU
vote for 705 members to represent them
in the Parliament.

Members of the European Parliament are called MEPs.
MEPs look at plans for new EU laws.
MEPs change the laws to make them better.
MEPs check if the EU is working as it should.

What the European Parliament does

The Parliament adopts new laws
for the EU with the Council.

The Council represents governments of countries
that are part of the EU.

The Parliament and the Council
also decide on the EU budget.

The Parliament checks the work
of all the organisations that help run the EU.

MEPs vote on trade agreements with countries
outside the EU.

How the European Parliament works

Groups

Most MEPs in the Parliament
work together in political groups.

Groups are made up of MEPs
from different countries
and different political parties.

The groups have common aims.

Committees

The Parliament has 20 permanent committees.
The biggest committee is the environment committee.
All MEPs work in the committees.

Committees come up with reports on
proposed new EU laws.

They propose improvements to laws.
MEPs on the committee vote on these proposals.

Plenary sessions

Then the whole Parliament votes
on new laws during plenary sessions.

Plenary sessions bring all MEPs together
to debate and vote on new laws.

MEPs elect a president
to represent the Parliament.

The president also chairs debates
and votes in the plenary session.

The president is elected for
two and a half years.

Cast your vote

You can vote for people to represent you in the
Parliament during the European elections.

European elections take place
every five years in all EU countries.

Stay informed

You can follow the work of the Parliament on social media.

The Parliament has at least one office in every EU country.
You can visit these offices.

Visit the European Parliament

You can visit the Parliament in Strasbourg
and Brussels.
